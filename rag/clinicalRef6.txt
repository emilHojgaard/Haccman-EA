Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

History: 
Reports dysuria, urinary frequency, and urgency for 2 days. No fever, flank pain, or hematuria.

Examination: 
Afebrile, abdomen soft, no costovertebral angle tenderness.

Investigations: 
Urine dipstick positive for leukocytes and nitrites. Urine culture pending.

Assessment: 
Uncomplicated urinary tract infection.

Plan: 
Prescribe short course of oral antibiotics. Encourage increased fluid intake. 
Advise follow-up if symptoms worsen or persist.
